Straight Kitchen

A general Straight kitchen design follows a single-wall kitchen layout with a counter space on both sides of the cooking range. Straight Kitchens are essentially meant for a small kitchen area. We can add certain element to a straight kitchen design including storage cabinets above and below. An arrangement for any straight kitchen design will match up to meet the needs of any user.

L Shaped Kitchen

When designing a functional kitchen, the ‘working triangle’ should be kept in mind - that is, the distance between your sink, stove and fridge. An L-shaped kitchen lends itself perfectly to this triangular tip, being two sides of a triangle to begin with. An L- shaped layout works best in small and medium sized spaces.

U Shaped Kitchen

A U-shaped kitchen is a highly coveted layout for a house hunter since they offer so much space for cabinetry. The u-shaped layout places units around the cook on three sides with the top of the ‘u’ left open for a doorway or an open plan living room. An open plan room often presents the opportunity to make one arm of the ‘u’ into a peninsula that can be accessed from both sides, such as for use as a breakfast bar or as a dual sided storage volume.

Cabinets

A kitchen consists of two parallel runs of units forming a central corridor in which to work. The parallel layout works well for all kitchen styles; it enhances safety and efficiency during cooking. It’s design optimize space by packing in an abundance of storage and work area, making them ideal for small kitchens. Though parallel kitchens work best in small spaces, they can also be good for medium-sized kitchens.
